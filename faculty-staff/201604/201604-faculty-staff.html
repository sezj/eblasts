April 26, 2016

Dear Colleagues,

This weekend, the first class to graduate under the college’s new name will receive their diplomas and head out into the world. I’m so grateful for the ways each of you has contributed to their excellent engineering education – I truly believe these graduates will transform the future.

It’s been a very exciting spring for the college. We had over 400 people attend our Engineering Leadership Summit, where we discussed the role of women in technology. We also launched the UF Innovation Station Sarasota County, the first engineering extension office of our Florida Engineering Experiment Station. We received $13.8 million this month from the legislature towards our new building – the Herbert Wertheim Laboratory for Engineering Excellence, which we will break ground on this fall.

The college is growing and our impact in the world is expanding. Read all about it below!

Best regards,

Cammy

UFTI Welcomes New Transportation Technology Transfer (T2) Center Director

Maria A. Cahill, AICP will take the position as the T2 Center Director in April 2016. She will lead the Center, which houses the Local Technical Assistance Program (LTAP), the Center for Transportation Training (CTT), Florida’s Pedestrian and Bicycling Safety Resource Center, the Florida Occupant Protection Resource Center, and several other dynamic grant-driven as well as revenue-supported programs. Cahill earned her B.A. in Political Science from the University of Florida, as well as her M.A. in Urban and Regional Planning with a focus on community planning, growth management and concurrency. She started her career as a local planner with Monroe County and later managed the Florida Keys Area of Critical State Concern program prior to moving to Tallahassee. Read more»
