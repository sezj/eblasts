Former UF Engineering dean Pramod Khargonekar to become Vice Chancellor of Research at the University of California, Irvine

Former UF Engineering dean Pramod Khargonekar

Khargonekar served as dean of the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering from 2001 to 2009. A UF alumnus, Khargonekar had a rich history in the college. He is known as a researcher, educator and national leader. He was most recently the Eckis Professor of ECE and assistant director of the NSF Engineering Directorate, which invests more than $900 million annually in frontier engineering research and education.

Al Carlson joins UF Innovation Station as Regional Director

Allen “Al” Carlson, retired CEO of Sun Hydraulics, joined the new UF Innovation Station Sarasota County as its regional director. In this position he will work closely with the Herbert Wertheim College of Engineering to serve the industry, residents and community of Sarasota County and the surrounding region by strengthening economic and workforce development opportunities.

STAFF SUCCESS

ESSIE was named an American Concrete Institute (ACI) Outstanding University for 2015. This award recognizes the school as being one of the top 16 programs in the nation with regards to ACI-related activities. This award is made possible by the diligent staff, faculty and students in ESSIE.


(From left) Amy Haberman, Yolanda Hankerson, Deb Mayhew and Paul Rocha were each selected for a 2016 Superior Accomplishment Award. This competitive and prestigious award is given to staff and faculty who go “the extra mile and beyond” in normal, assigned duties.

Two teams of engineering staff members (representing ESSIE and MSE) participated in the campus-wide 2016 Spring Walking Challenge. More than 25 engineering staff recorded their daily steps throughout the challenge and worked consciously toward living an active lifestyle. The MSE team ranked in the top 10 for achieving nearly 375,000 steps.

STUDENT SUCCESS

Naim Vilabrera, ESSIE and ELI Student Board Member, receives the J. Michael Rollo Diversity Impact Award. This award recognizes students who exemplify the value of diversity by actively contributing to and supporting multiple communities across campus.

Troy Gittelmacher, Jean-Ralph Avile, and Bernard Marger, CISE, competed in Microsoft’s U.S. Imagine Cup Competition. The team won the people’s choice award for its gun-safety device called GunLoc.
