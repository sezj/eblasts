Engineering Education Challenges in the 21st Century: The View from an Old Curmudgeon

Are you frustrated with ABET? Is there really a shortage of engineers? Do you think that there may be too much hype in the STEM frenzy? Will you be replaced by MOOCs? Are you happy educating the mythical T-shaped engineer? Do you carry a gun to class? Engineering educators in the 21st century face a number of challenges. Some are real, some are imagined and some are of our own making. Can we avoid political correctness for a while and question the accepted (or acceptable) group think?

Kenneth F. Galloway served as dean of Engineering at Vanderbilt from 1996 until 2012. His research and teaching activities are in solid-state devices and semiconductor technology, radiation effects in electronics, and engineering policy. Galloway has been elected a fellow of the IEEE, AAAS, and the American Physical Society. Galloway served as chair of the ASEE Deans Council (2009–2011) and was elected as an ASEE fellow in 2011. He is a member of the ASEE board of directors, and was president from 2013–2015. He currently serves as immediate past president.
